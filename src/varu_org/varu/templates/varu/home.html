Why Varu Network?

Restoring Hope Along the Varu River


"We make a living by what we get, but we make a life by what we give."

— Winston Churchill


Varu Network takes its name from the Varu River, which flows through the remote Khumi Chin communities of Tap Chaung village in Paletwa Township, Chin State, before continuing to Myaung Oo in Rakhine State. The Khumi Chin are one of Myanmar's Indigenous ethnic groups with a rich cultural heritage—their traditions, including distinctive large earrings and community celebrations that honor important achievements and milestones, reflect a strong sense of identity, unity, and deep respect for their customs.

Along the river's banks, villages have long faced significant barriers to education, healthcare, and sustainable livelihoods due to geographic isolation. Today, these challenges are compounded by the ongoing crisis in Myanmar. Many families are caught in the midst of armed conflict, with limited access to essential healthcare, disrupted education, and declining livelihood opportunities. Children have been forced out of school, while preventable illnesses continue to claim lives because medical services are distant, under-resourced, or inaccessible.

Varu Network is a newly established grassroots, non-profit initiative founded by a group of educators and young professionals who originate from these communities. As they continue to develop their own knowledge, leadership, and professional skills, they are united by a shared commitment to give back to their homeland and create lasting opportunities for future generations. Although Varu Network is at the beginning of its journey, our vision is long-term. We are committed to working alongside local communities and our growing initiative to ensure that no child is left without an education and no family is denied access to basic healthcare simply because of where they live.We are actively building a strong base of supporters who share this vision.

Together, we can help the communities along the Varu River move from survival to opportunity, and from crisis to hope.

Our work focuses on two critical areas: Education and Healthcare.
