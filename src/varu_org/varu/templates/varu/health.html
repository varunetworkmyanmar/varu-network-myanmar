Access to Healthcare Along the Varu River

For families living along the Varu River, reaching a hospital is often a journey measured not in kilometers, but in hope, endurance, and time. Geographic isolation, limited infrastructure, and the ongoing crisis in Myanmar have left thousands of people without access to timely medical care. When a child develops a high fever, a mother goes into labor, or an elderly person struggles to breathe, getting to a doctor is often the greatest challenge. Conditions that could be treated with timely care can quickly become life-threatening simply because help is too far away.


When Distance Becomes a Matter of Life and Death

For many villages, the nearest public hospital is Sittwe General Hospital, located in Sittwe, Rakhine State. Reaching it requires traveling by boat along the Varu River and followed by a ride in a small taxi—a journey that can take up to two days, depending on river levels and weather conditions.



Another option is Samee Tyke-Nell Hospital in Samee Town. However, for many communities, there are no roads and no ambulances. Instead, seriously ill patients must be carried to the hospital by family members and volunteers. Walking across steep hills, muddy paths, and remote terrain, the journey can take an entire day. For someone suffering from a severe illness, a serious injury, or pregnancy complications, every hour can mean the difference between life and death.

When a Community Becomes an Ambulance

When someone falls critically ill, an entire village responds. Without emergency transport, neighbors gather together to carry patients on makeshift stretchers or hammocks. They take turns bearing the weight over difficult terrain, crossing rivers and forest paths while carrying food, water, and supplies for the long journey ahead. These acts of compassion reflect the remarkable strength and solidarity of these communities. Yet they also reveal a heartbreaking reality: no family should have to carry a loved one for hours—or even an entire day— simply to reach basic medical care.

Bringing Healthcare Closer

No one should lose their life because they live too far from a hospital. By bringing essential healthcare services closer to communities along the Varu River, we can reduce dangerous delays, treat illnesses before they become life-threatening, and ensure that families receive the care they need—when they need it most. Access to healthcare is more than a service. It is a lifeline, and every community deserves the chance to reach it.
