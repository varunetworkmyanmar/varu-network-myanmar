Education at the Varu River


"Every child deserves a chance to learn, to dream, and to build a brighter future."

Advocating for Education

Education is one of the greatest challenges facing the communities along the Varu River. For many children, access to quality learning has been limited for years by geographic isolation and has become even more difficult due to the ongoing crisis in Myanmar.

Many villages lack safe and adequate school buildings, leaving children to learn in temporary shelters or community spaces that are not designed for education. Schools often have limited teaching materials, insufficient textbooks, and few learning resources, making it difficult to provide a quality learning environment. At the same time, they face a severe shortage of qualified teachers, leaving many classrooms dependent on volunteers with little or no formal training.

The Teacher Shortage

The shortage of qualified teachers is another critical challenge. Due to the remoteness of the region and the ongoing conflict, trained teachers are rarely available to serve these communities. To prevent education from coming to a complete halt, local communities have relied on dedicated volunteers and local residents to teach children. While their commitment has helped keep education alive, many have not received formal teacher training and have limited access to teaching resources and professional development.

Supporting these local teachers is equally challenging. The economic impact of the conflict has significantly reduced household incomes and livelihood opportunities. Many families struggle to meet their basic daily needs and are unable to contribute even modest school fees or teacher stipends. As a result, community schools often operate with little or no sustainable funding, placing the future of children's education at constant risk.

Why It Matters

Without sustained investment, today's educational challenges could have lasting consequences for future generations. Children who are unable to complete their education face fewer opportunities to pursue higher education, develop professional skills, or contribute to the long-term development of their communities.

At Varu Network, we are at the beginning of our journey. Our focus is to help communities keep education alive by:


  • Providing basic learning materials and resources
  • Supporting local volunteer teachers with training opportunities
  • Building capacity for community‑led education
  • Improving learning environments where possible
The Urgency

Education Cannot Wait

We cannot afford to wait. The longer children stay out of school, the harder it becomes to bring them back. By investing in education today, we invest in a brighter future for the Varu River communities.
