The Reality

Conflict Zones Without Schools

In conflict-affected areas across Myanmar, schools have been destroyed or turned into shelters. Children are growing up without access to basic education, leaving them vulnerable to exploitation and without the skills they need to build a better future.

  • Over 1,000 schools destroyed in conflict zones
  • 500,000+ children displaced from their education
  • Teachers forced to flee their communities
The Barrier

Poverty Keeps Children Out of School

For millions of families living in poverty, education is a luxury they cannot afford. Children are forced to work instead of learn, continuing the cycle of poverty that has trapped their families for generations.

  • Families can't afford school fees and supplies
  • Children work to support their families
  • Girls are disproportionately affected
